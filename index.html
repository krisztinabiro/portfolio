KultERP

A DMS for cultural institutions

Note:
This software is crafted and designed in Hungarian, specifically tailored for a unique client.
The prototype below provides a partial preview.

Link to the prototype

Problem

A new legislation has made it mandatory for cultural institutions, such as theaters, to utilize complex Document Management Systems.

The main challenge of this project was to ensure that our desktop application is as user-friendly as possible, while also complying with the strict laws.


Process


Studying the legislation

Initially, we outlined the basic functionality of the application. As it needed approval from the authorities, we had to adhere to the rules specified in recent legislation. This required us to carefully examine the relevant laws and prepare the app's specification accordingly.
Throughout this phase, I worked closely with our business analyst and legal consultant to delineate the application's features. Additionally, I played a significant role in preparing the requirements specification.


Preparing sketches

I have begun the design process by creating sketches. These sketches include low-fidelity versions of the screens, which enable me to explore various layouts and user flows.


User testing

We conducted multiple rounds of user testing with our clients to ensure that the app meets their needs and facilitates a seamless transition from their current system. The continuous user testing provided valuable feedback and allowed me to reconsider my design choices several times, making the design process iterative.

The key insights:

  • Initially, I employed a nearly white background for the screens. However, after receiving feedback from prospective users who found it too bright and obtrusive, I made the decision to switch to an ivory background.

  • At first, I implemented multilevel dropdown menus. However, based on the feedback received during testing, it became apparent that they contributed to more complex and extended user journeys. Additionally, since a majority of our prospective users are administrators utilizing a limited number of functions, I made the decision to simplify the user journeys. This involved linking each menu item directly to a frequently used function.


Solution

By closely collaborating with clients and studying the legislation, I have designed a user-friendly interface with meaningful icons that make navigation easy. Additionally, I have reduced the learning curve of the application by considering the existing DMS system used by the theater.

UI decisions


  • Skeuomorphic buttons

    • I've opted for skeuomorphic buttons in response to the client's request for an intuitive design, complemented by a paper-like background.
    I believe that employing salient buttons will further enhance the interface intuitiveness.


  • Accordion

    • Given that one of the client's primary requirements was to eliminate the need for scrolling, and considering that certain pages involve extensive forms, I have chosen to implement accordions.
    This decision aims to meet the specified requirement and streamline the interface by condensing lengthy forms into a more user-friendly format.


  • Clarifying icons

    • To enhance the recognizability of call-to-action and navigation elements, I've incorporated a variety of clarifying icons into my design.
    Given the software's intended purpose for everyday work use, I believe that embedding glyph icons within the input fields and accentuating icons in the menubar significantly eases perception, thereby enhancing the overall efficacy of the software.

Problem

Learning characters is one of the most challenging aspects of studying the Chinese language for foreigners.

📒 I designed and prototyped this app as part of my Bachelor's thesis, focusing on the associative learning techniques of Chinese characters, particularly in a digital environment.


Process


Testing learning apps and studying academic literature

During the research phase, I took a deep dive into various learning apps like Chineasy, Anki, and Quizlet. The goal was to pick up useful design tricks and spot areas where these apps could improve. Additionally, I delved into academic literature on character teaching and learning techniques, helping me shape the core methodology of my app, which revolves around associative learning principles.


Preparing sketches

Having solidified the core idea for the learning method, I proceeded to sketch out the essential features of the app.

UI decisions
